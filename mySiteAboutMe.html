Who I Am

Hi, I'm Paul — a graduate of the University of California, Berkeley, where I earned bachelor's degrees in Economics and Statistics. My undergraduate education gave me a strong foundation in quantitative analysis, econometrics, statistical modeling, and data-driven decision-making, while also teaching me how to approach complex problems with discipline and curiosity.

I'm especially interested in the intersection of economics, data, and technology — how markets behave, how incentives shape outcomes, and how modern statistical methods can be used to uncover meaningful insights. Through coursework and independent projects, I've worked with regression analysis, data visualization, modeling techniques, and programming tools that support analytical research and practical problem-solving.

Beyond technical skills, I value persistence, adaptability, and continuous improvement. I enjoy learning new systems, refining ideas, and building projects that combine clean execution with real utility. Whether I'm exploring economic trends, developing analytical tools, or creating digital products, I'm motivated by the process of turning ideas into something useful and well-crafted.
